§1The Problem

A massive, commerce-only market

Vietnam has 70 million monthly TikTok users (officially disclosed at TikTok Apps Summit SEA, October 2025). The country is the #4 TikTok Shop market globally by GMV — an estimated $6.5-7 billion in 2025 (YouNet ECI + Metric.vn), up 93% year-over-year. TikTok Shop holds ~40% of Vietnam's platform e-commerce, surpassing Shopee for the first time in active seller count (266K+ vs 209K). Vietnamese users spend 60-80 minutes daily on TikTok.

The single most important structural difference: TikTok's Creator Fund does NOT exist in Vietnam. Creators cannot earn from views. Every đồng comes from commerce — Shopee/TikTok Shop affiliate commissions, brand deals, livestream sales. This means every video is not "content" — it's a money-making or money-losing event. A good hook with the right format and pacing = orders, commissions, revenue. A bad hook with a stale format = 500 views, zero orders, a wasted day of filming. In the US, a bad video loses views. In Vietnam, a bad video loses income. This is why Vietnamese creators need to know EXACTLY why one video succeeds and another fails — not as intellectual curiosity, but to pay rent next month. Over 100,000 creators are active on Vietnamese TikTok, 60% of TikTok Shop revenue comes from affiliate creators — and most of them are guessing.

Every current solution fails

Doomscrolling as "research." Most creators do the same thing every morning: open TikTok, scroll for hours, screenshot good videos, copy links into Google Sheets, try to mimic what worked. Next day, repeat from scratch. No system, no data, no understanding of why one video got 2 million views while another got 500.

TikTok courses. Creators spend 3-5M VND on courses. After 3 months, the algorithm changes and the knowledge is stale. The Vietnamese TikTok course market has a documented trust deficit — VietNamNet investigations exposed repackaged free content sold at premium prices, fabricated revenue screenshots, and bait-and-switch pricing. Money wasted, time wasted, and still no answer to "what should I film today."

ChatGPT. Many creators pay $20/mo for ChatGPT Plus and ask "what hooks are trending in skincare?" ChatGPT generates plausible-sounding answers backed by zero actual TikTok data. No real videos, no real view counts, no way to verify.

Kalodata / Shoplus. Kalodata ($38-110/mo USD, Chinese-origin) and Shoplus ($39-79/mo USD) show which products are selling — but cannot answer the most important question: WHY that video sold. What kind of hook? What opening? What pacing? These are commerce tools, not creative tools.

The gap

No Vietnamese-language TikTok creative intelligence tool exists. LightReel ($199/mo), Foreplay, Atria — all English, USD-priced, built for US/EU ad workflows. Vietnamese creators earning 10-50M VND/mo from affiliate will pay for a tool that directly increases their income, if it speaks Vietnamese, understands Vietnamese niches (review đồ gia dụng, làm đẹp, Shopee affiliate, hài phương ngữ), and backs every recommendation with real data from real videos.

§3The Solution

GetViews is a Vietnamese-language chat interface that answers one question: "Làm sao kiếm nhiều tiền hơn từ TikTok — và nên quay gì tiếp theo?"

How it works

A daily batch job pre-analyzes the top-performing Vietnamese TikTok videos across 17 niches (§12 Intelligence Layer — ~255 videos/day, ~450/niche/month). When a user asks a question, GetViews analyzes their video or carousel on-demand using Gemini vision, then compares it against this pre-indexed corpus. No live fetching of reference videos, no CDN dependency for references. The user's video is the only thing downloaded in real-time. Carousel posts (image slides) are also fully supported — each slide analyzed individually with interleaved labeling for positional context. Results return in 20-30 seconds, entirely in Vietnamese, grounded in real videos with real view counts.

The intelligence compounds. Every day the corpus grows, niche norms sharpen, hook effectiveness rankings update, format lifecycles recalibrate, and emerging creators surface automatically. Month 1 GetViews has ~7,600 analyzed videos. Month 6 has ~46,000. Month 12 has ~92,000. The same question asked in Month 6 returns a fundamentally better answer than Month 1 — backed by more data, tighter niche benchmarks, and longer trend baselines. No course, no ChatGPT session, and no amount of doomscrolling can replicate a compounding data asset.

Show, don't just tell. Every response that references corpus videos includes visual thumbnail cards — the hook key frame, creator handle, view count, and TikTok link. When GetViews says "dựa trên 400+ video tháng này," the user sees the actual frames. Tap any thumbnail → opens the original TikTok. This makes the statistical authority tangible and verifiable — the opposite of ChatGPT hallucination.

The landing page IS the demo. A live input field on the hero section lets anyone paste a TikTok URL or type a question and see a real result — before signing up. The product sells itself. Free Soi Kênh (Intent ③) is available with zero signup as the primary conversion tool: paste any creator URL, get a structural analysis of their recent videos in 60 seconds. Courses can't do this. Agencies charge millions for it. See §15 for full landing page copy.

Why This Matters — Real Video Analysis, Not Caption Inference
The frame screenshots and audio transcripts above come from actual Gemini vision analysis of the downloaded video — not from TikTok captions or metadata. Captions often say generic things like "Review nồi chiên không dầu #review #shopee" — they don't tell you that the first frame has no face, that the text overlay is missing, or that the creator's first word comes at 2.1 seconds. This is the product thesis: hooks happen in the video, not in the caption. Without real video access, any "hook analysis" is educated guessing. Kalodata, Shoplus, and metadata-only tools cannot do this. Neither can ChatGPT.

System design: request lifecycle + best practices

Full Request Lifecycle — every message, traced
Step 1 — Client sends message
Next.js client → Vercel API route /api/chat. Payload: message text, session_id, Supabase JWT.

Step 2 — Auth + intent detection (Vercel, <200ms)
Verify Supabase JWT. Load session context (last 3 messages). Run intent classifier:
• Rule-based first pass: URL → ①③④, "brief"/"viết" → ⑤, "xu hướng"/"trend" → ⑥, "tìm KOL" → ⑦, else → follow-up
• Ambiguous → Flash-Lite text call (~$0.0003) with session context
• Conservative default: ambiguous = follow-up (free)

Step 3 — Credit check (Vercel, <50ms)
If deep intent: atomic credit decrement via Supabase RPC — UPDATE credits SET deep_remaining = deep_remaining - 1 WHERE user_id = $1 AND deep_remaining > 0 RETURNING deep_remaining. If returns 0 rows → insufficient credits → show upgrade prompt. Atomic operation prevents race condition from multiple tabs.

Step 4 — Route to backend
• Text-only intents (⑤⑥⑦ + follow-ups): Vercel API route → Gemini API directly → stream response via Vercel Edge Runtime (supports streaming, unlike serverless functions). Latency: 3-10s.
• Video-touching intents (①③④): Client opens SSE connection directly to Cloud Run endpoint https://getviews-api-xxxxx.run.app/stream with JWT + session_id. Cloud Run validates JWT against Supabase JWT secret (shared env var). Vercel never proxies video intents — avoids 60s timeout. Latency: 20-60s streamed.

Step 5 — Cloud Run processing (video intents only)
a. Fetch metadata via ED → get video URL
b. Download video via proxy (15s timeout — if exceeded, return "Video không tải được, thử lại sau")
c. Gemini Flash-Lite extraction (face timing, overlays, pacing)
d. Corpus lookup: query video_corpus for same niche, last 30 days, top by ER
e. Gemini Flash synthesis: user's extraction + corpus norms + knowledge base → Vietnamese output
f. Stream tokens to client via SSE as they generate
g. On complete: store full response in Supabase chats table, fs.unlink() downloaded video

Step 6 — Client receives + displays
SSE tokens render incrementally in chat UI. Thumbnail cards (corpus references) render after synthesis is complete — they reference R2 URLs which are static, not streamed.
